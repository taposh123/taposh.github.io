About me

I am a fourth-year PhD student at the MIT Operations Research Center, advised by Professor Dimitris Bertsimas. I am interested in new applications of mixed-integer optimization in transportation, machine learning, and public policy. I am currently part of a large collaboration led by Dimitris Bertsimas to develop impactful analytics to combat the spread of the Covid-19 pandemic. With Cindy Barnhart and Julia Yan, I am designing optimization tools to support MIT's pandemic planning for the 2020-2021 school year.

Over the last three years, I have worked on developing algorithms for routing school buses and optimizing school start times. With Dimitris Bertsimas and Sebastien Martin, we won first place in the Boston Public Schools Transportation Challenge in June 2017. You can read about the project in our paper, our SIAM News blog post or visualize BPS school buses in action here. Along with Boston Public Schools we were finalists for the 2019 Franz Edelman Award.

In the summer of 2019, I was a research intern in the Google OR team in Cambridge, MA, working with Ross Anderson and Christian Tjandraatmadja on reinforcement learning methods for combinatorial problems.
